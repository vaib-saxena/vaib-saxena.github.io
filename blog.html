Recursion

Move the mouse around to experience recursion

Recursion explained for a 5 year old

Answer

I will provide an example of recursion that will suffice the curiosity of a 5 year old and make him understand recursion.

Example 1


more

Karan has just arrived at his grandfather's place after six months. In these six months, a lot of houses(sixteen in total) were built around his grandfather's house and he couldn't recall where his friend Tushar lived. He goes on to ask his grandfather who has a a very witty answer. His grandfather says that he will guide him to Tushar's house but in a very different manner so as to save time and energy.

Step 2



There are sixteen houses in total and they start walking, Karan's grandfather asks him to make a guess, "Where do think is Tushar's house?", Karan answers by pointing and saying "Somewhere on the right". Karan's grandfather then says, "No, its not on the right row" and tells him that its on the opposite side i.e. the left row. (for visual convenience and ease we will not consider houses on the right row and will just hide them ) He then repeats the same question and asks him to make a guess again, "Where do think is Tushar's home in this left row?". Karan points at sixth house and says, "That one". Karan's grandfather again says, " No, not that one but you are close". Next, Karan's grandfather tells him not to consider the houses 1 to 4 while making the next guess. Karan's grandfather repeats the same question to guess Tushar's house among houses 5, 6, 7 and 8. Karan points at house 7 and tells it to his grandfather. His grandfather tells him that he is very very close and tells him not to consider house 5 and 6 in the next guess and probably the final guess.

Step 3



He then repeats the same question and asks him to make a guess again, "Where do think is Tushar's home in this left row?". Karan points at sixth house and says, "That one". Karan's grandfather again says, " No, not that one but you are close".

Step 4



Next, Karan's grandfather tells him not to consider the houses 1 to 4 while making the next guess. Karan's grandfather repeats the same question to guess Tushar's house among houses 5, 6, 7 and 8. Karan points at house 7 and tells it to his grandfather. His grandfather tells him that he is very very close and tells him not to consider house 5 and 6 in the next guess and probably the final guess

Step 5



This time Karan gets it right since he knew that 7 wasn't the right choice and points at house 8 and meets his friend Tushar. This iterative process of breaking down the problem by repeatedly doing the a same process as done by Karan's grandfather is what is called recursion. This process can even work on problems when there are 100 houses or more. This method could be explained to a 5 year old.
